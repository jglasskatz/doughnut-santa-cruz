# Santa Cruz, California — Context for Doughnut Portrait

## City Overview

- **Population**: ~65,000 (city); ~270,000 (county)
- **Area**: 15.8 sq mi (city)
- **Location**: Central California coast, ~75 miles south of San Francisco
- **Key institutions**: UC Santa Cruz (~19,000 students), Cabrillo College, Dominican Hospital
- **Economy**: Education, tech (growing remote/satellite offices), tourism, agriculture (county), retail
- **Government**: Council-Manager form of government

## Key Issues & Challenges

### Housing Crisis
- Severe affordability crisis driven by limited supply, geography (mountains/ocean), tech economy spillover
- ~34% of households are cost-burdened (>30% income on housing)
- Significant homelessness (214 persons in point-in-time count)
- Median home prices far exceed what local workers can afford

### Climate & Environment
- **Climate Action Plan 2030**: 40% GHG reduction below 1990 levels by 2030
- 2019 per capita emissions: 4.22 MT CO2e (down from 5.53 in 2005)
- Transportation = 69% of emissions
- Wildfire risk in surrounding mountains
- Coastal erosion and sea level rise threats
- CZU Lightning Complex fire (2020) devastated Santa Cruz Mountains

### Water Supply
- 95% surface water, 5% groundwater
- Sources: San Lorenzo River, North Coast creeks, Loch Lomond Reservoir (2.8 billion gallons)
- Live Oak well system (4 wells) for groundwater
- Drought vulnerability — Loch Lomond designed for single year's supply
- Member of Central Coast Community Energy (clean electricity)

### Biodiversity
- Global biodiversity hotspot
- 1,000+ native plant species, 17 endemic to county
- 35 endemic species (Ben Lomond wallflower, Ohlone tiger beetle)
- Old-growth redwood forests, Santa Cruz sandhills, karst caves, coastal prairie
- ~1,300 acres open space + 696 acres parks within city (43 parks)
- County: 32% protected land

### Agriculture (County)
- Pajaro Valley: major agricultural region (strawberries, lettuce, apples)
- ~1 million lbs pesticides applied annually (county-wide), 67% fumigants
- Pajaro Valley groundwater: 87% of nitrate contamination from agriculture
- City itself has minimal agricultural land

### Social Equity
- Significant disparities between affluent west side and lower-income areas
- UC Santa Cruz student population creates unique housing/economic dynamics
- Growing Latino/a community, particularly in south county
- Tech economy creating gentrification pressures

## Important Notes on Geographic Scale

Many datasets are available only at **county level**, not city level. When using this data:
1. Always note the geographic scale (city vs county vs region)
2. City of Santa Cruz is ~24% of county population — county averages may not reflect city conditions
3. South county (Watsonville/Pajaro Valley) has very different demographics and economy than the city
4. Some indicators (air quality, water quality) are monitored at stations that may or may not be within city limits
