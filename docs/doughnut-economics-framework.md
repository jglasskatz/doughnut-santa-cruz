# Doughnut Economics Framework Reference

## Overview

Doughnut Economics is a conceptual framework developed by Oxford economist **Kate Raworth**, first presented in her 2012 Oxfam paper *A Safe and Just Space for Humanity* and elaborated in her 2017 book *Doughnut Economics: Seven Ways to Think Like a 21st-Century Economist*.

The framework reimagines economic success: instead of pursuing endless GDP growth, the goal is for humanity to **meet the needs of all people within the means of the living planet**.

The visual model is a doughnut (or ring) shape. Between the two concentric rings lies the **safe and just space** where humanity can thrive.

## The Two Rings

### Social Foundation (Inner Ring) — 12 Dimensions

Below the social foundation, people fall short on life's essentials. These 12 dimensions are derived from the UN Sustainable Development Goals (2015):

| # | Dimension | Description | Key Indicators (examples) |
|---|-----------|-------------|--------------------------|
| 1 | **Food** | Access to sufficient, affordable, safe, and nutritious food | % food insecure households, food desert access |
| 2 | **Health** | Access to affordable, quality healthcare from first to last day | Life expectancy, uninsured rate, mental health access |
| 3 | **Education** | Access to quality education at all levels | Graduation rates, literacy, achievement gaps |
| 4 | **Income & Work** | Adequate income and decent work for all | Poverty rate, unemployment, living wage gap |
| 5 | **Water & Sanitation** | Access to clean water and sanitation | Water quality violations, access rates |
| 6 | **Energy** | Access to clean, affordable energy | Energy burden (% income on energy), renewable share |
| 7 | **Networks** | Access to information and social support networks | Internet access, social capital measures |
| 8 | **Housing** | Access to adequate, affordable housing | Cost-burdened households (>30% income), homelessness |
| 9 | **Gender Equality** | Equal opportunities regardless of gender | Gender pay gap, representation in leadership |
| 10 | **Social Equity** | Reduced inequality across race, class, and other dimensions | Gini coefficient, racial equity indices |
| 11 | **Political Voice** | Meaningful participation in political decisions | Voter turnout, civic engagement measures |
| 12 | **Peace & Justice** | Safety, access to justice, and freedom from violence | Violent crime rate, incarceration rate, trust in institutions |

### Ecological Ceiling (Outer Ring) — 9 Planetary Boundaries

Beyond the ecological ceiling, critical natural systems face degradation and potential tipping points. Based on the planetary boundaries framework by Johan Rockström and Will Steffen:

| # | Boundary | Description | Global Status |
|---|----------|-------------|---------------|
| 1 | **Climate Change** | GHG emissions trapping heat, destabilizing climate | OVERSHOOT — CO2 at ~420 ppm (boundary: 350 ppm) |
| 2 | **Ocean Acidification** | CO2 absorption making oceans more acidic | AT RISK — threatening marine ecosystems |
| 3 | **Chemical Pollution** | Release of toxic materials reducing biodiversity and fertility | OVERSHOOT — novel entities boundary exceeded |
| 4 | **Nitrogen & Phosphorus Loading** | Excess fertilizer causing algal blooms and dead zones | OVERSHOOT — both N and P boundaries exceeded |
| 5 | **Freshwater Withdrawals** | Excessive water extraction damaging ecosystems | AT BOUNDARY — green water boundary exceeded |
| 6 | **Land Conversion** | Converting natural land to human use | OVERSHOOT — especially tropical forests |
| 7 | **Biodiversity Loss** | Species extinction and ecosystem degradation | OVERSHOOT — well beyond safe boundary |
| 8 | **Air Pollution** | Particulate and chemical air contamination | VARIABLE — regional differences |
| 9 | **Ozone Layer Depletion** | Destruction of stratospheric ozone layer | RECOVERING — success of Montreal Protocol |

## The Safe and Just Space

The area **between** the two rings is where humanity can thrive:
- **Below the social foundation** = shortfall (people's needs unmet)
- **Beyond the ecological ceiling** = overshoot (planetary systems destabilized)
- **In the doughnut** = the safe and just space for humanity

No country currently meets all 12 social foundations without overshooting ecological boundaries (as of 2018 analysis of 150 countries).

## Key Principles

1. **Regenerative by design** — not just sustainable, but actively restorative
2. **Distributive by design** — sharing value creation more equitably
3. **Embedded economy** — economy is embedded within society, which is embedded within the living world
4. **Growth agnostic** — success is not measured by GDP growth but by thriving within the doughnut
5. **Systems thinking** — recognizing complex interconnections and feedback loops
